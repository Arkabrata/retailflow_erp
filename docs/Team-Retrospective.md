## 1. Team Overview

The RetailFlow project was developed by a five-member team following Agile Scrum practices across two sprints. Each team member contributed across design, development, testing, and documentation activities, with responsibilities distributed based on strengths and sprint priorities.

---

## 2. What Went Well

- Clear sprint goals helped the team stay focused on delivering working software.
- Incremental development ensured a functional system at the end of each sprint.
- Strong collaboration between backend and frontend development.
- Early completion of core features reduced risk in Sprint-2.
- Continuous testing helped identify issues early.
- Version control and tagging ensured clean release management.

---

## 3. What Did Not Go Well

- Initial underestimation of UI effort during Sprint-1.
- Limited time for automation testing due to project scope.
- Some features required refactoring during Sprint-2 due to evolving requirements.

---

## 4. Improvements Implemented in Sprint-2

- Better sprint planning and clearer backlog prioritization.
- Improved UI consistency and usability.
- Increased focus on integration testing.
- Better documentation and clearer commit messages.

---

## 5. Lessons Learned

- Agile development benefits significantly from early feedback.
- Delivering small, working increments reduces risk.
- UI/UX should be considered early, not as a final step.
- Documentation is critical for final evaluation and handover.
- Team communication plays a major role in sprint success.

The experience strengthened understanding of Agile practices, collaboration, and iterative delivery.
